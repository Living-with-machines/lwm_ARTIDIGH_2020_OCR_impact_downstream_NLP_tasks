Slow melting strains their Queen's approach declare Where'er she turns the Graces homage pay With arms sublime, that float upon the air, In gliding state she wins her easy way O'er her warm cheek and rising bosom movi The bloom of young Desire and purple light of Love. II. i. Man's feeble race what ills await ! Labour, and Penury, the racks of Pain, Disease, and Sorrow's weeping train, And Death, sad refuge from the storms of Fate ! The fond complaint, my song, disprove, And justify the laws of Jove. Say, has he given in vain the heavenly Muse ? Night and all her sickly dews, Her spectres wan, and birds of boding cry, He gives to range the dreary sky Till down the eastern cliffs afar Hyperion's march they spy, and glittering shafts of war.
