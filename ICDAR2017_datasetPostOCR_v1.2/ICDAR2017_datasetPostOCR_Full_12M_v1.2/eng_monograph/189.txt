In either hand the hastening Angel caught Our lingering parents, and to the eastern gate Led them direct, and down the cliff as fast To the subjected plain then disappeared. They, looking back, all the eastern side beheld Of Paradise, so late their happy seat, Waved over by that flaming brand the gate With dreadful faces thronged and fiery arms. Some natural tears they dropped, but wiped them soon The world was all before them, where to choose Their place of rest, and Providence their guide. They, hand in hand, with wandering steps and slow, Through Eden took their solitary way. - Paradise Lost, xii. 637-64g CLXXIX. FROM THE ELEGY ON COWLEY.
