THE SEVEN DEADLY SINS. 39 And in his hand his Portesse still he bare, That much was worne, but therein little redd For of devotion he had little care, Still drowned in sleepe, and most of his daies dedd Scarse could he once uphold his heavie hedd, To looken whether it were night or day. May seeme that wayne was very evil lecld, When such an one had guiding of the way, That knew not whether right he went, or else astray From worldly cares himselfe he did esloyne, And greatly shunned manly exercise From everie work he chalenged essoyne, t For contemplation sake yet otherwise His life he led in lawlesse riotise, By which he grew to grievous malady For in his lustlesse limbs, through evill guise A shaking fever raignd continually. Such one was Idlenesse, first of this company. And by his side rode loathsome Gluttony, Deformed creature, on a filthie swyne. His belly was upblowne with luxury, And eke with fatnesse swollen were his eyne And like a Crane his necke was long and syne, With which he swallowed up excessive feast, For want whereof poore people oft did pyne And all the way, most like a brutish beast, He spued up his gorge, that all did him cieteast. In greene vine leaves he was right fitly clad, For other clothes he could not weare ior heate And on his head an yvie girland had, From under which fast trickled downe the sweat. Still, as he rode he somewhat still did eat, And in his hand did beare a bouzing can,
