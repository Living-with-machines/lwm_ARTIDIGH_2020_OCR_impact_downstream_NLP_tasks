The blasts of Autumn drive the winged seeds Over the earth, - next come the snows, and rain, And frosts, and storms, which dreary Winter leads Out of his Scythian cave, a savage train. Behold ! Spring sweeps over the world again, Shedding soft dews from her aethereal wings Flowers on the mountains, fruits over the plain, And music on the waves and woods she flings, And love on all that lives, and calm on lifeless things. Oh Spring ! of hope, and love, and youth and gladness, Wind-winged emblem ! brightest, best, and fairest ! Whence comest thou, when, with dark Winter's sadness, The tears that fade in sunny smiles thou sharest ? Sister of joy ! thou art the child who wearest Thy mother's dying smile, tender and sweet Thy mother Autumn, for whose grave thou bearest Fresh flowers, and beams like flowers, with gentle feet, Disturbing not the leaves which are her winding-sheet. - Shelley.
