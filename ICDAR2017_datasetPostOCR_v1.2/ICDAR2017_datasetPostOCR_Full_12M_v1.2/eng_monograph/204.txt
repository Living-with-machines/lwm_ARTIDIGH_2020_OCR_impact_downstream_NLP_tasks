Softly sweet, in Lydian measures, Soon he soothed his soul to pleasures War, he sung, is toil and trouble Honour but an empty bubble Never ending, still beginning, Fighting still, and still destroying If the world be worth thy winning, Think, O think it worth enjoying. Lovely Thais sits beside thee Take the good the gods provide thee. The many rend the skies with loud applause So Love was crowned but Music won the cause. The prince, unable to conceal his pain, Gazed on the fair Who caused his care, And sighed and looked, sighed and looked, Sighed and looked, and sighed again At length, with love and wine at once oppressed, The vanquished victor sunk upon her breast. 6.
