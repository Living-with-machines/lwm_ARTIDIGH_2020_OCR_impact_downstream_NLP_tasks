[OCR_toInput] BK. I. AN ENGLISH ANTHOLOGY. 110 XCVII. EARLY LOVE. Ah, I remember well - and how can I But evermore remember well ? - when first Our flame began, when scarce we knew what was The flame we felt when as we sat and sighed And looked upon each other, and conceived Not what we ailed, yet something we did ail, And yet were well, and yet we were not well, And what was our disease we could not tell. Then would we kiss, then sigh, then look and thus In that first garden of our simpleness We spent our childhood. But when years began To reap the fruit of knowledge ah, how then Would she with sterner looks, with graver brow, Check my presumption and my forwardness ! Yet still would give me flowers, still would show What she would have me, yet not have me know ! 1615. - Daniel. XCVIIl SHALL I, WASTING IN DESPAIR.t Shall I, wasting in despair, Die because a woman's fair ? Or make pale my cheeks with care 'Cause another's rosy are ? Be she fairer than the day Or the flowery meads in May -If she think not well of me, What care I how fair she be ? From Hymen's Triumph, a Pastoral Tragi-Comedy . t This is from the original edition of Withers Fidelia, T6T5 of which there is a copy in the Bodleian Library vide Ward's English Poets, vol. ii .
