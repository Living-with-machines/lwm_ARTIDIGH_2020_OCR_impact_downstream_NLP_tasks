[OCR_toInput] SECT. I. 385 EVENING. Of Venice did not fall below her birth, Venice, the eldest child of Liberty. She was a Maiden City, bright and free No guile seduced, no force could violate And when she took unto herself a mate, She must espouse the everlasting Sea. And what if she had seen those glories fade, Those titles vanish, and that strength decay, -Yet shall some tribute of regret be paid When her long life hath reached its final day Men are we, and must grieve when even the shade Of that which once was great is passed away.
